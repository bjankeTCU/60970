Featured Project: An Examination of the Evolution of the Green Lantern

View Project

"In brightest day, in blackest night, no evil shall escape my sight. Let those who worship evil's might beware my power… Green Lantern's light!"

The Green Lantern oath may sound “hokey” to some, but it has endured as a symbol of power, will, and moral duty. From Alan Scott’s mystical green flame in the Golden Age to Hal Jordan’s intergalactic sci-fi patrols in the Silver Age, the Green Lantern legacy has constantly evolved to reflect the cultural values of its time. Alan Scott’s magic-born heroism offered wartime escapism rooted in myth, while Hal Jordan’s transformation into a cosmic peacekeeper mirrored society’s fascination with science, order, and growing social unrest.

My research explores how the Green Lantern evolved alongside American culture, from Alan Scott’s magical origins to Hal Jordan’s role in tackling real-world issues like civil rights and authority and reveals the deeper story of how American comics grew up with their readers. Making it web-friendly taught me how to simplify complex ideas using clear headings, conversational tone, and layout design to keep it engaging and accessible.
