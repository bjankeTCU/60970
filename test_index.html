Beware My Power: The Evolution of the Green Lantern

by: Beth Janke

"In brightest day, in blackest night, no evil shall escape my sight. Let those who worship evil's might beware my power… Green Lantern's light!"

The Green Lantern oath may sound “hokey” to some, but it has endured as a symbol of power, will, and moral duty. From Alan Scott’s mystical green flame in the Golden Age to Hal Jordan’s intergalactic sci-fi patrols in the Silver Age, the Green Lantern legacy has constantly evolved to reflect the cultural values of its time. Alan Scott’s magic-born heroism offered wartime escapism rooted in myth, while Hal Jordan’s transformation into a cosmic peacekeeper mirrored society’s fascination with science, order, and growing social unrest. This site explores how Green Lantern’s evolution from magic to science and from individual justice to institutional critique reveals the deeper story of how American comics grew up with their readers.
